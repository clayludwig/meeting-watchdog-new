About Meeting Watchdog

Over the last 10 years, we’ve watched local newsrooms quietly shutter. Some more have recently popped up, too, but this has left a gap in reporting around the United States. Local government meetings go uncovered and reporters are required to wear more hats each day.

That’s why the Howard Center for Investigative Journalism at the University of Maryland created Meeting Watchdog, an application that summarizes and catalogues local government meetings. Each meeting by each government in Maryland and Mississippi is organized by the date, committee and subject matter. There are summaries of the meetings, as well as transcriptions.

We used YouTube and Granicus APIs to create AI-generated summaries and transcriptions. Artificial intelligence is not perfect, nor do we expect it to be. Any information in this tool should be cross-checked and listened to in order to ensure that transcriptions and summaries are accurate.

We’ll continue adding updates, so please explore and contact for any feedback, inquiries or just to chat.
