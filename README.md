## Meeting Watchdog

This tool is an application created by students at the Howard Center for Investigative Journalism at the University of Maryland. It’s intended to meet a gap in reporting and make journalists’ jobs easier as local news outlets have shrunk in recent years.

It includes YouTube and Granicus files to Mississippi and Maryland local government meetings. These recordings are pulled through APIs, which are then organized into categories by date, committee and location. From there, summaries and transcripts are generated by artificial intelligence automatically in drop downs on the screen. These allow users to jump to certain timestamps in a meeting based on the transcript or summary for easy use.

We cannot assume that each summary or transcript is correct. Artificial intelligence is not perfect and will sometimes get things wrong. No quotes or facts should be pulled directly from the transcripts or summaries, but instead cross-checked against the cited recordings.
